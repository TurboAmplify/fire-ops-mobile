# Payroll & Tax Disclosure Hardening

## What's already in place
The payroll UI itself has the short disclaimer "Estimated Withholding — Not Official Tax Calculation" in five spots:
- Payroll page banner (`src/pages/Payroll.tsx`)
- Paystub on-screen modal (`src/components/payroll/Paystub.tsx`)
- Paystub PDF footer (`src/components/payroll/generatePaystubPdf.ts`)
- Withholding settings card (`src/components/payroll/PayrollSettingsCard.tsx`)
- Per-employee withholding form (`src/components/payroll/WithholdingProfileForm.tsx`)

## What's missing (the actual risk)
1. **Terms of Use** has no payroll/tax clause. If a contractor misfiles taxes off a FireOps paystub, the limitation-of-liability section is too generic.
2. **Privacy Policy** doesn't mention that payroll figures (rates, withholdings, deductions) are stored.
3. **Marketing site** (`fireopshq.com`) Terms/Privacy will mirror whatever the app has — same gaps.
4. **App Store description / review notes** don't disclose that the app produces estimated paystubs. Apple reviewers are sensitive to anything resembling financial/tax software.
5. **First-time payroll user** never sees a one-time acknowledgment that this is an estimation tool.

## Plan

### 1. Strengthen the in-app Terms of Use (`src/pages/Terms.tsx`)
Add a new section **"Payroll & Tax Estimation"** stating:
- Payroll figures (gross, withholdings, net, paystubs) are operational estimates only
- They are **not** official tax calculations, not generated by a licensed payroll provider, and do **not** replace W-2s, 1099s, 941s, or any IRS/state filing
- The user is responsible for filing taxes through a qualified payroll provider or CPA
- FireOps HQ is not liable for under/overpayment, penalties, or filing errors arising from use of these estimates
- Withholding percentages are simplified flat rates, **not** IRS tax tables

### 2. Update Privacy Policy (`src/pages/Privacy.tsx`)
Add payroll data to the "What we collect" list:
- Hourly rates, H&W rates, withholding profiles, dependents, filing status, deductions
- Stored only within your organization, accessible only to org admins
- Never shared with third parties or used for tracking

### 3. Add a one-time payroll acknowledgment
First time an admin opens the Payroll page, show a non-dismissable-until-accepted dialog:
> "FireOps HQ payroll is an **estimation tool** for operational planning. It is not a licensed payroll service. Figures are not official tax calculations. You remain responsible for filing taxes through a qualified payroll provider. By continuing, you acknowledge this."

Store acceptance in `localStorage` keyed by `org_id + user_id` so each admin sees it once.

### 4. Strengthen the existing in-app banner copy
Current: "Estimated Withholding — Not Official Tax Calculation."
Updated: "Estimated payroll for operational use only. Not a licensed payroll service. Not tax advice. Consult a CPA or payroll provider for filing."

Apply consistently across:
- Payroll page banner
- Paystub modal
- Paystub PDF footer
- Settings card
- Withholding form

### 5. Update App Store review notes (`docs/app-review-notes.md`)
Add a "Payroll Module" paragraph telling Apple's reviewer:
- The payroll feature is internal estimation only
- The app does **not** process payments, taxes, or money movement
- No financial accounts, no bank integration, no IRS filing
This pre-empts Guideline 4.0 / 5.1.5 questions.

### 6. Document for the marketing site
Update `docs/pre-submit-checklist.md` and append a new short doc `docs/marketing-site-sync.md` listing the **exact text blocks** to copy into the marketing site Terms and Privacy pages — same wording as the app, so legal language matches across surfaces.

When you switch to the marketing site project, you can use `@FireOps HQ` to pull these blocks verbatim.

### 7. Optional but recommended: Paystub PDF cover stamp
Add a faint diagonal "ESTIMATE — NOT OFFICIAL" watermark to the paystub PDF. Removes any ambiguity if a paystub gets emailed to a third party.
